The Art Program

Process over product. Connection over perfection.

Our weekly art sessions are designed with one guiding philosophy: the goal is never a finished painting. The goal is a child who tried something new, felt safe doing it, and left with a little more confidence than they arrived with. Art is the medium — connection is the outcome.

Who We Serve

🧩

Autism Spectrum

We create low-pressure, sensory-friendly environments where each child can engage at their own pace.

💛

Down Syndrome

One-on-one support helps each student explore materials and build fine motor skills through play.

🗣️

Communication Differences

Art gives a voice where words aren't always the easiest path — every mark on paper is communication.

🌱

Emotional Regulation

The rhythm of making art provides structure, calm, and a healthy outlet for big feelings.

How Sessions Work

Sessions are held weekly at our FCSN partner location in California. Each session runs about an hour and includes both 1-on-1 support and small group activities, depending on each child's needs. Materials are provided. No experience or preparation is needed from students or families.

Volunteer With Us

No art experience needed. Just patience, warmth, and a few hours a week.

🤝

What You'll Do

Sit with students, encourage exploration, help with supplies, and follow the session lead.

🕐

Time Commitment

Weekly sessions, roughly 2 hours including travel. We ask for at least a one-month commitment.

🎓

Who Can Apply

High school students and adults welcome. No prior experience with special needs required.
